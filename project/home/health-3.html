Cost Savings: Part Three

    Conclusion

    The model’s results revealed the usefulness of the state health and hospital spend per capita metric in determining the relative benefit of an ETHAN program to states across the U.S. From the thirty one states modeled they saw a combined benefit of $11.5 Billion (or 4.9% of total health & hospital spend in 2024). The implementation of additional improved care operating systems could see additional cost-benefits for the U.S. healthcare sector in which the highest net spend in the world could see transformed change in providing access to care.

    Implementation Challenges

    The main challenges in implementing Telehealth programs on a large scale is the cost of implementation which ranges from $15,000 to $50,000+. However the eventual savings that would be experienced, simulated by the study as $570,000 to EMS providers would recoup the initial funds spent on implementation.

    Policy Implications

    Enacting the ETHAN program in states with lower health and hospital expenditure per capita will see increased percentage savings compared to states with higher health and hospital expenditure per capita. I would recommend enacting the ETHAN system alongside other cost saving health systems in states with higher health and hospital expenditure per capita to maximize cost savings for those states’ populace. Alternatively, in states with lower health and hospital expenditure per capita an ETHAN telehealth system implementation will bring relatively higher cost savings for its populace.

    Final Thoughts

    Telehealth can enhance the lives of individuals through lowering costs. Saved cost could then be redirected to other health technologies creating a spiralling effect of reducing overall healthcare costs to patients and providers.
