  • Project date: 23 April, 2019

Research Project

Utilized the finite difference method to solve the Black-Scholes-Merton equation, calculating options prices via Implicit, Explicit, and Crank-Nicolson Methods. Compared Binomial Cox-Ross-Rubinstein Model to finite difference methods. Concluded that Explicit, Implicit, and Crank-Nicolson Methods aligned with Black-Scholes values, and the Crank-Nicolson method showed faster convergence. The finite difference model is favored over the Binomial CRR model for options pricing.
